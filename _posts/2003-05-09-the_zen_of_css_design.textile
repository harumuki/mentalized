In a time where XHTML/CSS is being bashed from <a href="http://www.livejournal.com/users/jwz/193866.html" title="CSS is BS">left</a> and <a href="http://www.aquasition.net/archive/2003/05/03/css_where_art_thou_height_auto.html" title="CCS: Where Art Thou?">right</a>, mezzoblues <a title="css Zen Garden: The Beauty in CSS Design" href="http://www.mezzoblue.com/zengarden/">css Zen Garden: The Beauty in CSS Design</a> is a much appreciated initiative.

The garden showcases some of the big strengths (and some of the big weaknesses) of purely CSS-based designs. It shows exactly how flexible your solution can be, when you separate content fand presentation.

Newsflash: The Garden even <a href="http://www.delorie.com/web/lynxview.cgi?url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.mezzoblue.com%2Fzengarden%2F" title="LynxView: CSS Zen Garden">works well in Lynx</a>.
